ENT Mathematics (School Program)



The course "Mathematics: Preparation for ENT" is designed for high school students preparing for the Unified National Testing (ENT). The course program covers all the necessary material of the school curriculum, including algebra and geometry. The main goal of the course is to systematize students' knowledge, develop problem-solving skills for typical tasks, and increase confidence before the test.

Course Content

Algebra

- Linear expressions and their properties
- Fractional expressions and transformations
- Solving linear equations
- Application of quadratic equations

Geometry

- Points, lines, and planes
- Angles and their measurement
- Areas of plane figures
- Volumes of solids

Trigonometry

- Trigonometric functions
- Trigonometric equations
- Trigonometric identities
- Trigonometric functions and graphs

Progressions
Logarithms and Exponential Equations
Combinatorics and Probability
Elements of Analysis
Practical Part

- Studying past ENT tests
- Conducting self-check tests
- Solving typical tasks on selected topics

Software and Resources

- Platform: Google Classroom
- Resources: Course website, YouTube math channels, textbooks

Class Planning
- Regular classes (2-3 times a week)
- Homework to consolidate material
- Conducting mock tests at the end of each module

Learning Format
- Lectures and practical classes.
- Homework with subsequent analysis.
- Regular mock tests simulating real ENT conditions.
- Individual consultations with the teacher.

Learning Outcomes
- Students will confidently solve problems in all sections of the school mathematics program.
- They will master effective strategies for preparing and taking ENT.
- They will be able to independently analyze their mistakes and improve results.

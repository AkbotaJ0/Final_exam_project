Linear Algebra



The course "Linear Algebra" is designed for students of mathematical, technical, and natural science specialties. The program covers the basic concepts and methods of linear algebra, which form the foundation for many areas of mathematics and its applications. The main goal of the course is to provide students with solid knowledge and skills in working with linear structures that are necessary for solving practical problems in various fields of science and technology.

Course Content

Introduction

- Introduction to linear algebra.
- Basic concepts: scalar, vector, matrix.
- Applications of linear algebra.

Vectors and Vector Spaces

- Vectors and operations on them.
- Linear combinations, linear dependence and independence.
- Bases and dimensions.

Matrices

- Basic operations on matrices.
- Types of matrices: square, zero, identity, diagonal.
- Transposition of matrices.

Determinants

- Determinant of a matrix: concept and properties.
- Method of calculating determinants.
- Minors and cofactors.

Inverse Matrices

- Inverse matrix: definition and properties.
- Gauss-Jordan method for finding the inverse matrix.
- Applications of inverse matrices.

Systems of Linear Equations

- Solving systems of linear equations.
- Gauss method.
- Matrix method for solving systems of equations.

Matrix Rank

- Matrix rank: definition and properties.
- Methods of finding the rank of a matrix.
- Application of rank for analyzing systems of equations.

Linear Transformations

- Linear transformations: concept and examples.
- Matrices of linear transformations.
- Composition of linear transformations.

Euclidean Spaces

- Euclidean space: definition and properties.
- Scalar product.
- Orthogonality and orthonormal bases.

Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors

- Eigenvalues and eigenvectors: definition.
- Method of finding eigenvalues and eigenvectors.
- Diagonalization of matrices.

Orthogonal Transformations

- Orthogonal and unitary matrices.
- Properties of orthogonal transformations.
- Applications of orthogonal transformations.

Review and Exam Preparation

- Overview of key concepts.
- Solving typical problems.
- Answering students' questions.

Class Schedule
- Regular classes (2-3 times a week)
- Homework assignments to reinforce material
- Conducting mock tests at the end of each module

Learning Format:
- Lectures with theoretical material.
- Practical classes with problem solving.
- Homework assignments for independent work.
- Tests and quizzes to check understanding of material.
- Individual consultations with the instructor.

Learning Outcomes:
- Students will master the basic concepts and methods of linear algebra.
- Learn to solve systems of linear equations and apply matrix methods.
- Be able to perform operations with vectors and matrices.
- Be able to apply theoretical knowledge to solve practical problems in various fields of science and technology.

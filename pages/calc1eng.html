Calculus 1



The course "Calculus 1" is designed for first-year students in mathematical and technical specialties. The course program covers the basic concepts and methods of mathematical analysis, which are fundamental for further study of mathematics and its applications. The main goal of the course is to provide students with a deep understanding of the theoretical foundations of analysis and develop skills in applying them to solve practical problems.

Course Content

Introduction and Basics of Analysis

- Overview of the course and its goals
- Basic mathematical concepts and definitions
- Real numbers and their properties
- Overview of functions and their graphs

Limits and Continuity

- Definition of a function limit
- One-sided limits
- Basic limit theorems
- Continuity of a function and its properties

Function Differentiation

- Concept of a derivative
- Geometric meaning of a derivative
- Differentiation techniques
- Differentiation rules (sum, difference, product, quotient)

Applications of Derivatives

- Higher-order derivatives
- Maximum and minimum points
- Analysis of functions for monotonicity and convexity
- Applications of derivatives in optimization problems

Derivatives of Complex Functions

- Chain rule
- Derivatives of trigonometric functions
- Derivatives of inverse trigonometric functions
- Logarithmic and exponential differentiation

Trigonometric Functions

- Limits and derivatives of trigonometric functions
- Applications of trigonometric functions
- Inverse trigonometric functions

Integration

- Indefinite integral and its properties
- Basic integration methods (by parts, by substitution)
- Definite integral and its properties

Applications of Integrals

- Area under a curve
- Volumes of solids of revolution
- Arc length of a curve
- Surface area of revolution

Integration Techniques

- Special cases of integration
- Rational functions
- Trigonometric substitutions
- Integration by parts

Series and Sequences

- Limits of sequences
- Numerical series and their properties
- Convergence criteria for series

Differential Equations

- Basic concepts of differential equations
- Method of separation of variables
- Linear first-order differential equations
- Applications of differential equations in real-world problems

Review and Exam Preparation

- Review of main concepts and methods
- Solving typical problems
- Preparation for the final exam

Class Schedule
- Regular classes (2-3 times a week)
- Homework assignments to reinforce material
- Practice tests at the end of each module

Learning Format:
- Lectures with theoretical material.
- Practical classes with problem-solving.
- Homework assignments for independent work.
- Quizzes and tests to check understanding.
- Individual consultations with the instructor.

Learning Outcomes:
- Students will master the basic concepts and methods of calculus.
- They will learn to solve typical problems involving limits, derivatives, and integrals.
- They will be able to apply calculus to solve practical problems in various fields of science and technology.
- They will develop skills in logical thinking and rigorous mathematical proof.

Calculus 2



The course "Calculus 2" is a continuation of the course "Calculus 1" and is intended for students who wish to deepen their knowledge in the field of analysis. The course covers more complex and advanced topics such as differential equations, functions of several variables, integrals over curves and surfaces. The main goal of the course is to develop students' ability to apply the methods of mathematical analysis to more complex problems and prepare them for further study of related mathematical disciplines.

Course Content

Overview of Integral Calculus

- Review of the indefinite integral
- Fundamental theorem of calculus

Integration Methods

- Integration by parts
- Substitution method
- Integration of rational functions (partial fraction decomposition method)
- Integration of trigonometric functions
- Numerical integration methods (trapezoidal and Simpson's rules)

Applications of the Definite Integral

- Calculation of areas
- Calculation of volumes of solids of revolution
- Calculation of arc lengths and surface areas of revolution
- Calculation of centers of mass and moments of inertia

Improper Integrals

- Convergence and divergence of improper integrals
- Integrals with infinite limits of integration
- Integrals with discontinuous integrand functions

Sequences and Series

- Introduction to sequences and limits
- Geometric and arithmetic series
- Convergence criteria for series (absolute and conditional convergence)
- Taylor and Maclaurin series

Polar Coordinates

- Transition from Cartesian to polar coordinates
- Area and arc length in polar coordinates
- Parametric equations and their applications

Differential Equations

- Basic concepts and definitions
- Solving simple first-order differential equations
- Applications of differential equations in real-world problems

Review and Final Exam Preparation

- Review of key concepts and methods
- Solving typical problems
- Final exam preparation

Class Planning
- Regular classes (2-3 times a week)
- Homework to reinforce material
- Mock tests at the end of each module

Learning Format:
- Lectures with theoretical material.
- Practical classes with problem solving.
- Homework for independent study.
- Quizzes and tests to assess understanding.
- Individual consultations with the instructor.

Learning Outcomes:
- Students will master the methods of working with functions of several variables and their applications.
- Learn to solve basic types of differential equations.
- Will be able to apply integrals over curves and surfaces in various applications.
- Acquire skills in working with Fourier series and Fourier transforms.
